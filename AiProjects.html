02 — Data Science / Research

Surveillance
Research

A two-person research project investigating how U.S. law enforcement agencies use surveillance technologies and what that means for public privacy. Using four datasets, we built graphs and visualizations, applied SQL to find trends, and trained a Random Forest classification model to predict the probability of each technology being used by a given agency type. Model accuracy was evaluated using F1 scores for precision and recall.
